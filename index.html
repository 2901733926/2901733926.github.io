One-shot Voice Based on Enhanced Speaker Encoder and Postnet

Submitted to Applied acoustics

Huimin Wang,Ning Chen*

* Corresponding author.

Abstract

VQMIVC achieves promising results on one-shot voice conversion (VC) task. However, the speaker embedding extracted by VQMIVC suffers from information redundancy and information leakage, resulting in low quality of the converted speech. To alleviate this issue, we creatively introduce a simple, parameter-free attention module (SimAM) to force the speaker encoder to focus on the speaker information while compressing irrelevant information. In addition, the ConvNeXt block is introduced after the downsampling layer in the speaker encoder to extract more global and high-level speaker information while balancing the computational efficiency. Furthermore, since the postnet in VQMIVC is made up of several convolutional layers, it is ineffective at capturing the global features of the predicted mel-spectrogram. In order to further refine the predicted mel-spectrogram, we propose a transformer-based postnet as transformer has a powerful ability to capture global features. Finally, a feature fusion module is designed to efficiently exploit the complementary nature of the two postnets. Experiments show that our proposed model can significantly improve the quality, speaker similarity and naturalness while maintaining low computational complexity and time complexity when compared with VQMIVC and other state-of-the-art (SOTA) baselines.
