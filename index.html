One-shot Voice Conversion Based on Enhanced Speaker Encoder and Postnet

Abstract

VQMIVC achieves promising results on one-shot voice conversion (VC) task. However, since its speaker encoder suffers from information leakage, and the postnet cannot capture the global features of the predicted mel-spectrogram efficiently, the quality of the converted speech is unsatisfactory. To alleviate this issue, first, we introduce the simple, parameter-free attention module (SimAM) to make the speaker encoder focus on speaker-related information while compressing speaker-irrelevant information. Secondly, the ConvNeXt block is integrated into the speaker encoder to extract more global and high-level speaker representation while balancing the computational complexity. Finally, a Transformer-based postnet is fused with the original postnet to take advantage of both local and global features of the predicted mel-spectrogram to refine it towards the true mel-spectrogram. Experiments on VCTK dataset demonstrate that the proposed model outperforms VQMIVC and other state-of-the- art (SOTA) baselines in both objective and subjective evaluations while maintaining almost the same computational complexity as VQMIVC.

One-shot VC performance

Our proposed model uses the same training setup as VQMIVC[1], while SRDVC[2] and AGAIN-VC[3] use open-source pre-trained models.
